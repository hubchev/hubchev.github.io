DSIBM: Data Science for International Business Management (M.Sc.)

(FOR NON-DATA SCIENCE GRADUATES)

Author

Prof. Dr. Stephan Huber

Question Very short answer
For Whom? For B.A. who studied economics or business administration NOT for BA of data science or computer science
What is data science? “Data science is best understood as partnership between a data scientist and a computer.” (Kelleher and Tierney 2018, 97)
What is a computer? It is a data-processing machine that can extend the capacities of our brains.
What do students learn? How to use the computer professionally for business and scientific purposes.
How to use a computer professionally? Students learn programming languages, software tools, methods and procedures for using data to solve business problems.
Why study DSIBM? Buzzwords that are linked with DS such as machine learning, artificial intelligence or big data have been discussed heavily in recent years and even more since ChatGPT. Job perspectives are brilliant.
What makes DSIBM special? It is a M.Sc. for students who have no or little experience with programming, computer science, IT, database management, statistics, mathematics, and empirical methods. Thus, it is perfect for students who want to ride the wave and specialize in a field that comes with great(er) job opportunities. Other M.Sc. programs related to DS are focussing either on advance statistical mathematical methods or on programming.
Why has the HS Fresenius decided to offer a M.Sc. for non-DS grads? The strategy is to find (more) students and lecturer. So far, we don’t see many students with a bachelor degree in data science. Universities just recently started to offer study programs for DS. Moreover, wages paid for data scientist in the free market that could teach DS on a master level are above that what the HS can offer. Thus, we don’t have neither many students nor lecturer that can meet the standards of a M.Sc. in DS.
Is DSIBM applied? Yes, students will put their hands on the keyboard and learn writing code to manage data, visualize data, analyze data, communicate insights from data, and help making decisions in business using the computer.
How will the performance of students be measured? When it is more about theoretical concepts with exams and when it is more about how to apply methods and concepts, students should prepare case-studies, (poster) presentations, and academic papers. We also use take-home exams to test the ability of students to solve problems with the computer under time constraints.
Can students go abroad? Yes, the third semester contains only courses that are not necessary required for successfully studying DSIBM.
